Markov Chain Monte-Carlo (MCMC) Estimation of π

The idea is to estimate π by estimating the areas of a circle and a square and then using the ratio below to calculate π. By throwing random dots at the circle and the square you can estimate their respective areas by replacing "area of" with number of random dots that fell inside each shape. The more random dots you have thrown, the better your estimation of the areas, and the better your estimate of π becomes.

area of circle / area of square
= π r^2 / (2r)^2
= π / 4
